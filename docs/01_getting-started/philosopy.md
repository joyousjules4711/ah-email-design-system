# Writing Guidelines

This document defines the writing principles for all transactional email communication created as part of the Akademikerhilfe Email Design System.

The goal is to provide a consistent, friendly and professional communication style across all departments while supporting the Akademikerhilfe brand experience.

---

# Purpose

Transactional emails are not marketing messages.

They support students throughout their journey by providing clear information, guiding them through processes and helping them complete specific actions.

Every email should therefore be easy to understand, easy to scan and focused on the recipient's needs.

---

# Tone of Voice

The Akademikerhilfe tone of voice is

- professional
- friendly
- respectful
- approachable
- helpful
- trustworthy

We communicate confidently without sounding bureaucratic.

---

# Addressing Recipients

Use the formal German form ("Sie") for all external communication unless a strategic decision changes this in the future.

The same level of professionalism should be maintained in English.

---

# Writing Principles

## Write for People

Always write for the recipient—not for the system.

Avoid internal terminology whenever possible.

Explain processes from the student's perspective.

---

## Keep It Simple

Use short sentences.

Avoid nested clauses.

Prefer one idea per paragraph.

---

## Be Helpful

Anticipate the recipient's next question.

Whenever possible, answer it before it needs to be asked.

---

## Use Active Voice

Prefer

> Bitte vereinbaren Sie Ihren Einzugstermin.

instead of

> Ein Einzugstermin ist zu vereinbaren.

---

## Explain Before Warning

Always explain the normal process first.

Mention exceptions or consequences afterwards.

This creates a more positive reading experience.

---

## Focus on the Next Action

Every email should guide the recipient towards one clear action.

Examples

- Accept the offer
- Open the online portal
- Complete the payment
- Contact Studentservice

Avoid presenting multiple equally important actions.

---

# Language

Use plain language.

Avoid unnecessary legal or administrative wording.

However, legally required terminology must never be replaced.

Examples include

- Benützungsentgelt
- SEPA-Lastschriftmandat
- Vertragsbeginn

These terms should be explained where necessary, but not rewritten.

---

# Positive Communication

Whenever possible, formulate messages positively.

Instead of

> You have not...

prefer

> Please...

Instead of

> Failure to...

prefer

> If...

The objective is to guide—not to intimidate.

---

# Structure

Emails should follow a predictable structure.

1. Greeting

2. Purpose

3. Important information

4. Required action

5. Additional information

6. Contact information

7. Signature

This structure should remain consistent across templates.

---

# Headings

Use meaningful headings.

Examples

- Payment Options
- Your Online Portal
- Before Your Move-In
- Early Move-In

Avoid generic headings such as

- Information
- Details
- Miscellaneous

---

# Calls to Action

Buttons should contain clear action verbs.

Good examples

- Accept Offer
- Open Online Portal
- Upload Documents
- View Contract

Avoid

- Click Here
- Continue
- More

---

# Links

Links should describe their destination.

Prefer

> Open the Online Portal

instead of

> Click here

---

# Dates

Dates should always be written consistently.

German

10.06.2026

English

10 June 2026

---

# Currency

Always use

EUR 100,00

German formatting

or

EUR 100.00

English formatting

depending on language.

---

# Numbers

Use numerals for

- dates
- room numbers
- payment amounts
- deadlines
- references

Write small quantities as words where this improves readability.

---

# Email Length

Keep emails as short as possible.

Additional information should only be included if it supports the primary purpose.

---

# Visual Hierarchy

The most important information should appear first.

Use

- headings
- spacing
- buttons
- information panels

to guide attention.

Avoid large blocks of uninterrupted text.

---

# Consistency

The same process should always be explained in the same way.

Standard wording should be reused whenever possible.

This improves readability and reduces maintenance.

---

# Accessibility

Avoid writing in ALL CAPS.

Avoid excessive punctuation.

Avoid using colour as the only way to communicate information.

Links and buttons should remain understandable without surrounding context.

---

# English Communication

English emails should not be literal translations.

Instead, they should communicate the same meaning using natural English.

Consistency of meaning is more important than literal wording.

---

# Do

✔ Use short paragraphs

✔ Explain the next step

✔ Be friendly

✔ Be professional

✔ Keep language simple

✔ Reuse standard wording

✔ Focus on one primary action

---

# Don't

✘ Write like a legal document

✘ Use internal abbreviations

✘ Hide important information inside long paragraphs

✘ Use more than one primary call-to-action

✘ Overuse bold formatting

✘ Overcomplicate sentences

---

# Guiding Principle

Every email should answer three questions for the recipient.

> What happened?

> What do I need to do?

> What happens next?

If these three questions are answered clearly, the email has achieved its purpose.
